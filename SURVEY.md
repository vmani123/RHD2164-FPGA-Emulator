# SURVEY — lossless multichannel biosignal compression candidates

Cost-filtered, ranked survey of **lossless** compression methods for the 128-ch
RHD2164 / HD-EMG node (STM32H745 Cortex-M7 and Spartan-7 XC7S25; integer/fixed
only; causal/streaming; must beat per-channel FLAC by exploiting cross-channel
spatial correlation). **This file PROPOSES only** — no measured ratios, no codec
edits (non-negotiable #4). Watch-list methods are never promoted without explicit
human approval. Seeds `compression_spec/candidates.md`.

Embeddable: a few adds to accumulate neighbour |e| + a context→k lookup, backward-adaptive, RTL-trivial. Risk to measure: if the per-block k already tracks local variance well, the cross-channel context may add little — a clean, cheap test settles it._

   on its own samples, so its taps match that regime's local AR statistics instead of a single
   filter converging to a quiescent/burst compromise. Decoder mirrors the regime from causal
   reconstructed history → zero side-info; order stays ≤4 per P2. Why (information-theoretic):
   HD-EMG is strongly non-stationary and bursty; a single adaptive LMS UNDER-fits the
   high-variance bursts where most residual bits live. Regime-switched prediction lowers the
   CONDITIONAL residual variance H(e | regime) < H(e), lowering residual entropy UPSTREAM.
   **NOT the retired `xctx`** (that conditioned the Rice PARAMETER k on a cross-channel context,
   leaving the residual unchanged, and lost because H(e|neighbour energy)≈H(e) after LMS); this
   conditions the PREDICTOR COEFFICIENTS on a TEMPORAL activity regime, reducing the residual
   itself. **NOT** the retired entropy back-end swap (the coder stays adaptive Rice). Grounded in
   adaptive switching / context-dependent linear prediction (Seemann & Tischer; Ulacha
   MAE-minimizing context-switched prediction, Entropy 2024). Verdict **borderline → embeddable**
   (N×order-4 state + energy accumulator + regime compare; keep N small, 2–4). Distinct axis
   (temporal residual entropy) from #1/#3. INSIGHTS open-frontier #2. Risk: if order-4 already
   whitens the residual enough that per-regime AR statistics barely differ, the bank adds state
   for little gain — a cheap, clean test settles it.
3. **Scale-SELECTED two-stage spatial cascade (`acar?+lms4bp`, table I).** Salvage of cycle-10
   frontier #1, a distinct axis (spatial-scale meta-gating) but lower payoff. The always-on
   `acar+bestpartner` cascade helped only on tight 64-ch OTB (+0.81%) and was neutral/negative on
   the large 128/320-ch arrays (P1-refinement: the two MI slices are additive only where the
   global common-mode is a real eigenvector). Instead CHOOSE per recording between CAR-first vs
   best-partner-only, from a decoder-derivable global-vs-local coherence statistic (array channel
   count, or the ratio of array-mean energy to total residual energy over the first reconstructed
   block). Keeps the OTB max-ratio corner without the large-array regression, at zero side-info
   (the gate is a deterministic function of reconstructed data). Both stages are already shipped
   and verified — this is a per-recording meta-gate, not a new primitive → lowest mechanism risk,
   low-to-medium payoff (tight arrays only). Distinct axis (scale meta-gating) from #1/#2. INSIGHTS
   open-frontier #3, de-prioritized below #1/#2.

Order reflects expected value per unit embedded cost for THIS device on the real grids:
the one untried fusion that could STACK the two substitute spatial DOF into a win on both
array scales (#1) > a genuinely different axis (temporal residual entropy) now that the spatial
lever is saturated (#2) > a low-risk scale meta-gate that salvages the tight-array cascade corner
(#3). All three are genuinely distinct in mechanism (joint SELECTED-pair spatial predictor vs
regime-switched temporal predictor vs per-recording spatial-scale gating), not parameter variants
of each other, and none re-proposes a retired mechanism — the two touching retired territory (#1, #2)
name the prior attempt and why they differ.
